## What Exactly Are Microdramas?

A microdrama, also known as a vertical drama or short drama, is a serialized storytelling format specifically designed for mobile devices. Each installment typically lasts between one and two minutes, and the episodes are meticulously crafted to end on cliffhangers, encouraging viewers to continue watching. The narrative content often leans into popular, high-emotion tropes such as secret billionaires, revenge plots, arranged marriages, and supernatural romances. To access the full story, viewers usually watch the first few episodes for free and then purchase coins to unlock subsequent ones. This model effectively compresses traditional soap opera pacing into bite-sized, TikTok-style content, optimizing for viewer retention and in-app spending.

## The Explosive Growth of the Microdrama Market

The microdrama industry has witnessed exponential growth, transforming from a niche format originating in China into a global multi-billion dollar business. Deloitte projects that in-app microdrama revenue will reach approximately $7.8 billion in 2026, more than doubling from the previous year. Industry publications like Variety and The Wrap frequently refer to the vertical short-drama market as an eight-billion-dollar enterprise. This surge is evident in app store revenue, which saw a dramatic increase from roughly $178 million in Q1 2024 to nearly $700 million in Q1 2025.

Leading companies in this space include ReelShort and DramaBox. ReelShort, developed by the US-based Crazy Maple Studio, has successfully adapted the Chinese model for Western audiences, focusing on English-language originals. In contrast, DramaBox has scaled a vast library of licensed content globally. While ReelShort reportedly generated around $1.2 billion in gross consumer spending in 2025 with over 70 million monthly active users, operating at a deliberate loss to capture market share, DramaBox stands out as a profitable player, reporting $323 million in revenue and approximately $10 million in net profit in 2024. The success of these platforms offers a stark contrast to the failed long-form vertical video attempts like Quibi, demonstrating the viability of the short-form approach.

## AI's Role in Revolutionizing Production

The key to the microdrama boom lies in its production costs, and Artificial Intelligence is rapidly lowering these barriers. New AI tools can now generate scripts, storyboards, characters, and even finished vertical video from a single prompt, drastically reducing what was once a multi-day production process to mere hours. This technological advancement is paving the way for a new wave of AI-native studios. Even platforms like Character.AI are experimenting with AI-made microdramas, such as their c.ai series, which allows adult viewers to interact with characters after each episode. This integration hints at a future where microdramas merge with interactive AI experiences.

## The Emerging Frontier: Adult AI Dramas

While mainstream microdrama apps generally adhere to a PG-13 rating, their flirtation with desire and dramatic tension leaves a clear opening for adult-oriented content. This gap is being filled by platforms like Candy AI, which is pioneering the adult tier of microdramas. Candy AI combines the addictive, episodic nature of microdramas with its advanced AI companion engine. It delivers high-quality, story-driven adult scenes that viewers can then influence by chatting directly with the AI characters. This offers a personalized, interactive, and adult-focused take on the microdrama format, moving beyond static clips to create a more engaging narrative experience.

Other AI companion platforms, such as OurDream AI and DreamGF, are also exploring the convergence of generated imagery, short video, and interactive characters. However, Candy AI is notably positioning short-form adult drama as a primary format rather than an add-on.
